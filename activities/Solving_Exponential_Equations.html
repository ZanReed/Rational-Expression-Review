PROBLEM 18 — WORKED EXAMPLE
\(9^{2x+4} \cdot 9^{2x} = \dfrac{1}{81}\)
\(x =\)
Step 1 Apply the product rule: \(9^{2x+4} \cdot 9^{2x} = 9^{(2x+4)+(2x)} = 9^{4x+4}\)
Step 2 Rewrite \(\dfrac{1}{81}\) as a power of \(9\): \(\dfrac{1}{81} = \dfrac{1}{9^2} = 9^{-2}\)
Step 3 Equation: \(9^{4x+4} = 9^{-2}\)
Step 4 Set exponents equal: \(4x + 4 = -2\)
Step 5 Solve: \(4x = -6 \Rightarrow x = -\dfrac{6}{4} = \boxed{-\dfrac{3}{2}}\)
