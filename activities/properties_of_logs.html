PROPERTIES OF LOGARITHMS  |  Identify Each Property
Product
Multiplication becomes addition
\(\log_b(M \cdot N)\)  =  ?
\(\log_b M + \log_b N\)
\(\log_b M - \log_b N\)
\(n \cdot \log_b M\)
\(\log_b M \cdot \log_b N\)
When two values are multiplied inside a single log, split them into two logs with the same base added together.
Quotient
Division becomes subtraction
\(\log_b\!\left(\dfrac{M}{N}\right)\)  =  ?
\(\log_b M - \log_b N\)
\(\log_b M + \log_b N\)
\(\dfrac{\log_b M}{\log_b N}\)
\(n \cdot \log_b M\)
A fraction inside a log splits into the top log minus the bottom log — numerator first, then denominator.
Power
Exponent becomes coefficient
\(\log_b(M^p)\)  =  ?
\(p \cdot \log_b M\)
\(\log_b M + \log_b N\)
\((\log_b M)^{\,p}\)
\(\log_b M - \log_b p\)
The exponent on the argument drops in front as a multiplier (coefficient) — it does not go on the outside of the log.
