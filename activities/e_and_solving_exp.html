SECTION 2 — CONVERTING & SIMPLIFYING WITH ln
Natural log: ln x means \(\log_e x\)
A natural logarithm is a logarithm with base \(e\). It is written as \(\log_e x\), but usually as \(\ln x\). Conversion:  \(e^x = y \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \ln y = x\)

SECTION 3 — SOLVING EQUATIONS WITH e AND ln
Convert form → Solve → Round to nearest tenth → Check
1For ln = ln: use the One-to-One Property → set arguments equal.
2For ln = number: condense, isolate the log, then convert to e-form: \(\ln x = n \Rightarrow e^n = x\).
3For \(e^{(\ldots)} = \text{number}\): isolate the exponential, then take \(\ln\) of both sides: \(e^{(\ldots)} = n \Rightarrow (\ldots) = \ln n\).
4CHECK FOR EXTRANEOUS SOLUTIONS — arguments of ln must be positive.

SECTION 4 — APPLICATIONS OF NATURAL BASE e
Substitute → Compute → Round to nearest tenth
Like \(\pi\), e is a special irrational number that occurs in continuous exponential growth and decay. Its value is approximately \(e \approx 2.71828\ldots\) Growth: \(f(t)=ae^{kt}\) with \(k>0\).   Decay: \(f(t)=ae^{kt}\) with \(k<0\).
