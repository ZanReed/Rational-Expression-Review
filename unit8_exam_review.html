What is the domain of the function
\[ f(x) = \frac{7}{x^2+12x+35} \]
Your answer
Answer Factor the denominator: \((x+5)(x+7)=0\) gives \(x=-5\) and \(x=-7\).

What is the range of the function
\[ f(x) = \frac{1}{x-2} - 4 \]
Your answer
Answer The horizontal asymptote is \(y=-4\), so \(f(x)\) never equals \(-4\).
\(\text{Range: }(-\infty,-4)\cup(-4,\infty)\)

If \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x}\) is changed to \[g(x)=\tfrac{1}{4}\,f(x+3)-7\] how is the graph transformed?
Your answer
Answer The graph of \(f(x)\) is vertically compressed by a factor of \(\tfrac{1}{4}\), then translated 3 units left and 7 units down.
New asymptotes: vertical \(x=-3\), horizontal \(y=-7\).

What are the zeros of the function
\[ f(x)=\frac{x^3+6x^2-27x}{x-2} \]
Your answer
Answer Factor the numerator: \(x^3+6x^2-27x = x(x^2+6x-27) = x(x+9)(x-3)\)
The denominator \((x-2)\) shares no factors with the numerator, so \(x=2\) is a vertical asymptote, not a zero.
Set numerator \(= 0\):
\[\boxed{x=0,\quad x=-9,\quad x=3}\]
