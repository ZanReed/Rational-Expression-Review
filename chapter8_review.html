1 Simplify the following
a. Simplify
\[ \frac{42a^3}{49a^2} \]
Your answer
Answer GCF of 42 and 49 is 7. Subtract exponents on \(a\).
\[\frac{42a^3}{49a^2} = \frac{6a}{7}\]
b. Simplify
\[ \frac{x^2-10x+9}{x^2-1} \times \frac{x+4}{x^2-5x-36} \]
Your answer
Answer Factor each polynomial:
\(x^2-10x+9 = (x-1)(x-9)\)
\(x^2-1 = (x-1)(x+1)\)
\(x^2-5x-36 = (x-9)(x+4)\)
Cancel \((x-1)\), \((x-9)\), and \((x+4)\):
\[\frac{(x-1)(x-9)}{(x-1)(x+1)} \times \frac{(x+4)}{(x-9)(x+4)} = \frac{1}{x+1}\] Restrictions: \(x \neq 1,\,-1,\,9,\,-4\)
c. Simplify
\[ \frac{2x^2y}{3z^3} \div \frac{6y^3}{12xz^4} \]
Your answer
Answer Flip the divisor and multiply:
\[\frac{2x^2y}{3z^3} \times \frac{12xz^4}{6y^3}\] Multiply numerators and denominators, then simplify:
\[\frac{2 \cdot 12 \cdot x^3 y z^4}{3 \cdot 6 \cdot z^3 y^3} = \frac{24x^3yz^4}{18y^3z^3} = \frac{4x^3z}{3y^2}\]
2 Simplify the following
a. Simplify
\[ \frac{4}{k+1} + \frac{2}{k+2} \]
Your answer
Answer LCD \(= (k+1)(k+2)\)
\[\frac{4(k+2)}{(k+1)(k+2)} + \frac{2(k+1)}{(k+1)(k+2)}\] \[= \frac{4k+8+2k+2}{(k+1)(k+2)} = \frac{6k+10}{(k+1)(k+2)} = \frac{2(3k+5)}{(k+1)(k+2)}\]
b. Simplify
\[ \frac{3x+3}{5x-4} - \frac{3x-1}{5x-4} \]
Your answer
Answer Same denominator — subtract numerators directly:
\[(3x+3)-(3x-1) = 3x+3-3x+1 = 4\] \[\frac{4}{5x-4}\]
3 Analyze the function Determine values and describe the transformations
\[ f(x) = \frac{2}{\tfrac{1}{2}(x-12)} + 4 \]
a. Domain
b. Range
c. Asymptotes
Determine the values then describe the transformations:
d. \(a =\)
e. \(b =\)
f. \(h =\)
g. \(k =\)
Describe the transformations
Answer First simplify: \(\dfrac{2}{\frac{1}{2}(x-12)} = \dfrac{4}{x-12}\), so \(f(x) = \dfrac{4}{x-12}+4\)

a. Domain: \((-\infty,\,12)\cup(12,\,\infty)\)
b. Range: \((-\infty,\,4)\cup(4,\,\infty)\)
c. Vertical asymptote: \(x=12\)  |  Horizontal asymptote: \(y=4\)

d. \(a = 4\)   e. \(b = \tfrac{1}{2}\)   f. \(h = 12\)   g. \(k = 4\)

Transformations: The graph of \(f(x)=\tfrac{1}{x}\) is vertically stretched by a factor of 4, compressed horizontally by \(\tfrac{1}{2}\), shifted 12 units right, and shifted 4 units up.
4 Analyze the function
\[ f(x) = \frac{4+x}{12+x} \]
a. Domain
b. Range
c. Asymptotes
Answer Rewrite by long division: \(\dfrac{x+4}{x+12} = 1 - \dfrac{8}{x+12}\)

a. Domain: \((-\infty,\,-12)\cup(-12,\,\infty)\)
b. Range: \((-\infty,\,1)\cup(1,\,\infty)\)
c. Vertical asymptote: \(x=-12\)  |  Horizontal asymptote: \(y=1\)
(Degrees are equal and leading coefficients are both 1, so HA \(= \tfrac{1}{1} = 1\))
5 Simplify and determine any asymptotes or holes
a. Identify all asymptotes and holes
\[ f(x) = \frac{x^3+1}{x^2-4} \]
Your answer
Answer Factor denominator: \(x^2-4=(x-2)(x+2)\)
Factor numerator: \(x^3+1=(x+1)(x^2-x+1)\) — no shared factors with denominator.
Degree of numerator (3) > degree of denominator (2) → oblique asymptote, no HA.
Divide: \(x^3+1 \div (x^2-4)\) gives quotient \(x\), so oblique asymptote \(y=x\).

Vertical asymptotes: \(x=2\) and \(x=-2\)
Oblique asymptote: \(y=x\)
Holes: none
b. Identify all asymptotes and holes
\[ f(x) = \frac{(x-4)(x^2-4)}{x-8} \]
Your answer
Answer Expand numerator: \((x-4)(x^2-4)=(x-4)(x-2)(x+2)\)
No common factors with denominator \((x-8)\) — no holes.
Degree 3 numerator, degree 1 denominator → no HA, no oblique asymptote in the simple sense; the function grows without bound.

Vertical asymptote: \(x=8\)
Horizontal asymptote: none
Holes: none
c. Identify all asymptotes and holes
\[ f(x) = \frac{5}{(x-1)(x+7)} \]
Your answer
Answer Already fully simplified. Numerator degree (0) < denominator degree (2).

Vertical asymptotes: \(x=1\) and \(x=-7\)
Horizontal asymptote: \(y=0\) (numerator degree < denominator degree)
Holes: none
6 Direct variation word problem
Lisa's earnings vary directly with how many hours she babysits. If she earns $68 for 8 hours of babysitting, find her earnings after 5 hours of babysitting.
Your answer
Answer Direct variation: \(y = kx\)
Find \(k\): \(\;k = \dfrac{68}{8} = 8.50\) dollars per hour
Earnings for 5 hours: \(\;y = 8.50 \times 5 = \$42.50\)

Solve the equation:
\[ \frac{7}{3} - \frac{3}{x-5} = \frac{19}{12} \]
Your answer
Answer LCD of 3, \((x-5)\), and 12 is \(12(x-5)\). Multiply every term:
\[12(x-5)\cdot\frac{7}{3} - 12(x-5)\cdot\frac{3}{x-5} = 12(x-5)\cdot\frac{19}{12}\] \[28(x-5) - 36 = 19(x-5)\] \[28x - 140 - 36 = 19x - 95\] \[9x = 81\] \[\boxed{x = 9}\] Check: \(x=9 \neq 5\) ✓
